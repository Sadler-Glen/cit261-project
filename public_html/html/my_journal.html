My Journal

Week one was much to do with orientation and organizing a GitHub account and to learn about agile methodologies. The feeling at this stage was that we were going to work as a team on a project this semester. We would later find out that this was not strictly what was going to be. However, having read about agile methodologies and digested as best as I could, I came to realize that these real-life type methodologies for working in teams would be so helpful to understand for the work we would do this semester.

In the second week we met for the first time as a team. At this stage I was really confused and so were my fellow team mates. To make things worse for me is that I had not done a lot of JavaScript, CSS, or HTML since 2015 and my skills were very rusty. What was I thinking when I decided to do this course? We met on the Wednesday evening of the second week and to some degree, my feelings of unease subsided somewhat when I discovered how cool the people were that I was going to work with. Jayton was from Texas, Ali from California, and Ricardo from Peru. I instantly fell in love with my team. We managed to work through a teaching schedule together, dividing up the 13 topics for the semester, which, incidentally, had be taught by week 10. This meant that we had to teach more than one topic per week. Each of us were able to enjoy input and surprisingly this was not that painful. I was still not entirely sure that we understood how this semester was going to work, how eactly it was to be presented, and to what depth. One thing we did know was that it had to be presented online in the form of a web site.

As a team we felt we needed to get to it pretty smartly so Jayton boldly took on the first teaching assignment and we planned to meet on the Saturday afternoon for me, Friday night for everyone else, to kick off the teaching assignments. Jayton did a great job and I came to realize the caliber of our team was way up there. We were on our way in terms of the teaching assignments, however, for me the presentation of the topics was a bit hazy.

At this point I was presented with the new concept of GitHub Pages. I had previously used GitHub versioning with java from the previous semester so was somewhat familiar with GitHub. I decided to continue on from the previous semester of using NetBeans IDE as the basis for writing code and uploading to GitHub. I did not get familiar enough with Pages until week three so in week two I ended up using my personal site to provide my code to Brother Baer.

I think that this was my most difficult week – the not knowing was killing me and I thought that I was simply not going to get through the semester.

In week three it was my teaching assignment. I had started my preparation as soon as I chose the assignment in week two. Having not done a lot of coding since 2015, I had to dig deep to rediscover the things that I had previously learned. I am so grateful to my Heavenly Father for helping me out here for he truly brought to my remembrance those things which I needed to recollect. Things all changed for me in Week three. On the Friday of this week we met with Brother Baer and he unfolded for us the intent of the first three weeks of the course. Here, he explained, we were being thrown into a real-life scenario where often we are required to learn new things, practices, technologies, and methodologies by being thrown into the deep end. We get to learn the depth of our character and the character of those of a team when this happens. How we react to these situations is all important.

From this point on I started enjoying the journey. My entries into my journal from this week onwards started with comments like in week 4, “This week went pretty smoothly and I’m starting to enjoy how flexible this class is.” Week 5, Another really good week, was able to cover topics 6-8” Week 6, “Another great week, and another great meeting…” and so it went on.

I looked forward to our weekly team meetings. There was always an air of discovery shared and we came to enjoy the time that we shared together – what we had learned and what we were learning. In week six I recorded the following.

“We are moving along quite nicely and meeting our milestones that we set in week two. Everyone is contributing and looking to help each other which is really great. I am really, really impressed with the caliber of work that is being produced by the team. Our meeting this week was taken by Jayton - He's one smart cookie and so humble with it. I love who I'm working with and enjoying the way that this class is being run. All I can say is that it is Fantastic.!!!

By week six I had completed topics 1-8 and they had been marked and the feeling was exhilarating. I was on target to submit topics 9-10 and I found that not only were we on target with our team schedule, I was ahead of my personal schedule. In week seven I had managed to complete topics 11-13. I was excited that very soon I was going to be able to tackle the individual project.

The nature of the final project was not revealed until week eight where Brother Baer advised that the project would be a Mobile App and that we should be thinking about what that might be for each of us. He invited us to submit by email what our APP would be. The Rubric for the final project, not surprisingly, was to implement the topics that we had learned over the semester into the APP.

For the next three weeks I worked on the Weather APP. I chose to build my APP around the Openweather API which gathers data from weather stations globally and to also use google geolocation. The functionality that I wanted to build was:

  1. 1) On starting APP go to the current location using google geolocation.
  2. 2) Show the weather of the current location and a five-day forecast.
  3. 3) Input a location and display the current weather and a five-day forecast.
  4. 4) Also display a map of the location. (I did this after marking in Week 12)
  5. 5) Add animations and transitions that did not detract from the essence of the APP.

This was not without it’s difficulties but with the help of our team and a lot of trial and research I managed to get through to a product where Brother Baer, in week 11, was able to mark.

I will be eternally grateful for the blessings that I received from my Heavenly Father over this semester. I am so grateful for the opportunity to learn in the hallowed environment of BYU Idaho, and with a team that was second to none. Each member has blessed my life immeasurably and will always be remembered. Thank you, Brother Baer for your mentorship over this semester. This has been a wonderful learning experience. Another bonus came in the form of the new changes implemented by President Nelson and the Brethren. Our Heavenly Father and His Son Jesus Christ are indeed at the helm of this church. I bear testimony of this truth in the name of Jesus Christ amen.
